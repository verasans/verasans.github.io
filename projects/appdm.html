This project took around 3 months. It was a hard deadline since other features had more priority on the roadmap. Some details

doccle blue

Previously the doccle app had a different colour for each menu, which represented a huge challenge for our accessibility first approach. First things first! We needed to understand if these different colours were relevant to our docclers‚Äô navigation. According to the support team, and from the reviews from App and Play stores, we were able to understand that the users were always referring to the screen they were on by its title and not by its accent colour.

We then decided to go for one main colour on the doccle mobile application.
Blue was already a very much used colour on the doccle web app as well as on email communications and social media posts. So the challenge was only to find a similar tone that would fit light and dark mode in terms of readability and colour contrast. And "doccle-blue" was born! By doing this, we manage to keep the visual impact on our docclers to the minimum while unifying our user experience across our different platforms.


Top bar

This was a tough one since a lot of our stakeholders considered the dark grey top bar to have a doccle feeling and part of the branding style.

It took quite some iterations on this but, in the end, everyone was happy with a light top bar for light mode and a dark top bar for dark mode as long as we managed to keep the rainbow present.

The rainbow is a very important element of doccle branding since the beginning of the company. It‚Äôs present across all our communications, internal and external and it‚Äôs also part of the company logo.


Small changes, big impact

The previous design was the same for iOS and Android, so, by changing to a more native look & feel we‚Äôve reduced the learning curve and helped docclers feeling more confident to explore and use the doccle mobile app. By using native components we were also able to reduce implementation time and code maintenance. The headers and dropdown lists now have a more common feel for each OS and the background was changed from light grey to white, giving the app a more modern and light look.

‚ÄúNot finding the Archive page‚Äù was one of the main reasons for bad ratings on the App and Play stores. The solution was to change the dropdown into two tabs: Home and Archive. Under Archive docclers can find all their documents, while on Home there‚Äôs a first section with Urgent documents meaning documents that require any action from users like pay or e-sign. Filters

The major improvement on the Filters screen was the redesign of the tags. The flow of the screen was also improved, by having the search input followed by the date range input and only then by the tags. The tags section is composed by the Partners group, which is populated with all the companies that the user is connected with. Next, at the Categories group, we find the tags created by the user.

Previously, when a tag was selected, you could barely notice the difference, even if you are not a visually impaired person. To avoid this the tags have now icons before the labels that change depending on their select state. So now everyone can understand the difference between the two states of the tags, regardless of vision, device screen and tech-savviness. We also reduced the click error by making the tags bigger, in height and width.

The introduction cards are meant to give a first general overview of what is doccle and what you can do on the app. The illustrations were re-thought for a more accurate explanation of the doccle features and to get a more simple and modern look & feel. The use of the entire screen is to get users more focused on the explanation than on the dimmed background.
